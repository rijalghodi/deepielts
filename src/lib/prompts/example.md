### Revised Essay

Some people argue that children should begin their formal education at a very early age because it helps them develop essential skills like reading and writing sooner. They believe that this advantage can positively impact their academic performance later. Additionally, early education may instill discipline and enhance social skills within a structured environment.

Conversely, others contend that children should start school at an older age to have more time for emotional and physical growth. They believe that young children learn more effectively through play and interaction in informal settings, whether at home or in community environments. Starting school too early could lead to stress and diminish their enthusiasm for learning.

In my opinion, children should begin their formal education around the age of six. This timing allows them to be better prepared mentally and socially while still enjoying their early childhood experiences.

Furthermore, they suggest that early schooling can cultivate important life skills, including discipline, focus, and social interaction, which are crucial for success in later educational and social environments.

They contend that informal learning experiences contribute to creativity, curiosity, and a love of learning, which may be stifled by the pressures of formal education at too young an age. This perspective posits that delaying the onset of formal schooling allows children to develop holistically, both emotionally and socially.
